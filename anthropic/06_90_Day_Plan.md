# 90-Day Plan - Anthropic GTM Role

*Use this as a discussion document in interviews to demonstrate strategic thinking*

---

## Context

This plan assumes a GTM role focused on enterprise sales, potentially with APAC or financial services focus. Adjust based on actual role.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Week 1-2: Product & Company

**Objective:** Become dangerous on Claude capabilities

- [ ] Complete all internal Claude training/certifications
- [ ] Build personal projects with Claude API (hands-on, not just reading)
- [ ] Understand pricing, packaging, enterprise features deeply
- [ ] Learn competitive positioning vs GPT-4, Gemini, Llama
- [ ] Shadow 5+ customer calls to understand current sales motion
- [ ] Review win/loss analysis from last 2 quarters

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- What use cases does Claude win? Where do we lose?
- What's the typical deal cycle?
- Who are the buyers (technical vs business)?
- What objections come up repeatedly?

### Week 3-4: Market & Customers

**Objective:** Understand the landscape

- [ ] Map top 50 enterprise accounts (current + target)
- [ ] Identify patterns in successful deployments
- [ ] Understand partner ecosystem (SI, ISV, consulting)
- [ ] Learn regional nuances (if APAC role)
- [ ] Meet with customer success to understand post-sale challenges
- [ ] Review support tickets for common issues

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- Which industries show most traction?
- What's the land-and-expand motion?
- Where are the biggest whitespace opportunities?

### Deliverable: 30-Day Readout
- Observations on GTM strengths and gaps
- Initial hypotheses on opportunities
- Questions for leadership

---

## Days 31-60: Contribute

### Week 5-6: Quick Wins

**Objective:** Add value while still learning

- [ ] Take ownership of 3-5 active opportunities
- [ ] Bring in at least one new opportunity from my network
- [ ] Identify one process improvement (based on Day 1-30 observations)
- [ ] Start building relationships with key internal stakeholders (engineering, product, legal)

**For APAC focus:**
- [ ] Map the APAC opportunity (market size, key accounts, competitive landscape)
- [ ] Identify 10 anchor accounts to prioritize
- [ ] Assess need for local partners vs direct coverage

**For Financial Services focus:**
- [ ] Deep dive on regulatory requirements (SOC2, data residency, explainability)
- [ ] Map competitive positioning vs incumbents (Salesforce, Microsoft, etc.)
- [ ] Identify 5 lighthouse accounts that would drive industry credibility

### Week 7-8: Build Pipeline

**Objective:** Create future momentum

- [ ] Generate $X pipeline (TBD based on quota)
- [ ] Develop POC playbook for top 2 use cases
- [ ] Create competitive battle card for top competitor
- [ ] Host first customer workshop or event

**Deliverable: 60-Day Readout**
- Pipeline status
- Win/loss insights from first deals
- Recommendations for Q2 priorities

---

## Days 61-90: Lead

### Week 9-10: Scale What Works

**Objective:** Turn learnings into repeatable motions

- [ ] Document playbook for winning use case
- [ ] Train peers on what's working
- [ ] Propose territory/account strategy adjustments
- [ ] Identify hiring needs (if applicable)

### Week 11-12: Drive Strategy

**Objective:** Contribute to long-term GTM strategy

- [ ] Present market opportunity analysis to leadership
- [ ] Propose partner strategy (if gaps identified)
- [ ] Recommend product feedback based on sales conversations
- [ ] Set OKRs for next quarter

**Deliverable: 90-Day Readout**
- Results achieved
- GTM strategy recommendations
- Resource requests
- Personal development plan

## Success Metrics

### Quantitative
- Pipeline generated: $X (based on quota)
- Deals closed: X (may be early for full close)
- Win rate: X% (or trending positive)
- POCs launched: X

### Qualitative
- Trusted by engineering/product as voice of customer
- Invited to strategic discussions
- Peers asking for help/advice
- Clear hypotheses on GTM improvements
